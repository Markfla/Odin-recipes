Carbonara

Carbonara is a beloved Italian pasta dish known for its creamy, indulgent sauce and simple yet rich ingredients. Traditionally made with spaghetti or rigatoni, the sauce consists of eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, pancetta or guanciale, and black pepper. The hot pasta is tossed with the egg and cheese mixture, creating a silky, creamy coating without the need for cream, while the crispy pancetta adds a savory, smoky flavor. Originating from Rome, Carbonara is celebrated for its harmonious blend of textures and tastes. Each bite offers a perfect balance of creamy and salty, with a hint of peppery spice.
